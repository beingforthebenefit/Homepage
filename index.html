Welcome

Welcome to the home page of Gerald Todd. This website was built using a plain text editor, no external libraries or frameworks (with the exceptions of the linked projects), no inline CSS, and is totally BEMed out. It is desktop and mobile friendly. The site is also self-hosted on my local server. I admire simplicity, and I tried to reflect this in the design. I am color-blind, so forgive me if coolors.co generated an ugly color palette.

Dependencies worry me. A lot. Just ask these two million developers. I do everything in my power to write in vanilla Javascript/PHP/etc. for it helps me more deeply understand these languages, creates a smaller and more secure application, and is just cool. When I have to work with libraries and frameworks, a deeper understanding of the base code gives me a benefit.

About

Wall-mounted Raspberry Pi-powered infoboard

My career goals have always been in the areas of math, science, technology, and education. After getting a few degrees in Mathematics, I began working as a full-stack (LEMP) developer for a small company in Portland, OR. Programming is a requisite skill for advanced mathematics and is something I've become proficient with. I keep my skills sharp in my personal life by building my own computer, maintaining my own media server, and using SoCs to run my own DNS server and power a homemade wall-mounted infoboard (News, calendar, schedule, pretty pictures, etc.).

Technology

From when I first started building simple static webpages in HTML when I was 13, I have always had an obsessive curiosity with how technology works. Family friends heard I had a personal website, and I began getting approached to build websites to sell products, host pretty pictures, and for local political campaigns.

Now, I have many microcontrollers around my house automating various tasks, from an iMessage server (coded into a jailbroken iPhone 4) to receive iMessage on non-Apple devices, to filtered photoresistors that detect the color of the ambient light in the room and LEDs that match it.

Snow Crash is my favorite book. Vonnegut is my favorite author.

Teaching

I have steadily taught mathematics as a tutor since 2005, and taught courses at the university level since 2014. I earned my Master's degree in applied math, optimization, combinatorics and analysis. Education is a passion of mine as I have taken many graduate courses on the subject and taught university math courses since 2014. I have also won awards for being the top graduate student teacher. Through teaching and tutoring for the past 10 years, I have gained excellent skills working with other people and making firm (sometimes unpopular) decisions all while keeping a positive rapport. My leadership skills have been honed over the years through teaching at the University of Montana. In the group of teachers that had courses alongside mine, I was the only native English speaker. This meant that I was in charge of coordinating the courses. These responsibilities included:

  • organizing semiweekly meetings with all course instructors
  • laying out a plan for the structure of the courses
  • adapting that structure based on live feedback from the other instructors
  • organizing out-of-class events for students to provide extra help for those in need
  • maintaining the webpage
  • constructing the homework assignments for the courses
  • writing the exams for the courses
  • devising a grading rubric for the courses
  • proofreading all content by the various instructors

I excelled at all aspects of this job and even won an award for having the highest student evaluation of all graduate student teachers.
